Brief Overview of

The Roman Empire's Timeline

Founding and Kingdom Period (753 BC - 509 BC)
- 753 BC: According to legend, Rome is founded by Romulus and Remus.
- 753-509 BC: Rome is ruled by a series of kings, beginning with Romulus.

Roman Republic (509 BC - 27 BC)
- 509 BC: The last Roman king is overthrown, and the Roman Republic is established.
- 264-146 BC: The Punic Wars are fought against Carthage, leading to Roman dominance in the Mediterranean.
- 133-31 BC: Period of internal strife and civil wars, including the rise and assassination of Julius Caesar in 44 BCE.

Roman Empire (27 BC - 476 AD)
- 27 BC: Augustus (formerly Octavian) becomes the first Roman emperor, marking the beginning of the Roman Empire.
- 27 BC - 180 AD: The Pax Romana, relative peace and growth.
- 64 AD: The Great Fire of Rome occurs during the reign of Emperor Nero.
- 117 AD: The empire reaches its greatest territorial extent under Emperor Trajan.
- 235-284 AD: The Crisis of the Third Century, a period of military, political, and economic turmoil.

Decline of the Western Roman Empire (285 AD - 476 AD)
- 285 AD: Emperor Diocletian splits the empire into Eastern and Western regions for easier governance.
- 313 AD: Emperor Constantine issues the Edict of Milan, granting religious tolerance for Christianity.
- 330 AD: Constantine moves the capital to Byzantium, later renamed Constantinople.
- 410 AD: Rome is sacked by the Visigoths.
- 476 AD: The last Roman emperor, Romulus Augustulus, is deposed, marking the fall of the Western Roman Empire.

Byzantine Empire (Eastern Roman Empire) (330 AD - 1453 AD)
- 527-565 AD: Reign of Emperor Justinian, notable for codifying Roman law (Corpus Juris Civilis).
- 1054 AD: The Great Schism, the split between the Roman Catholic and Eastern Orthodox Churches.
- 1453 AD: Fall of Constantinople to the Ottoman Turks, marking the end of the Byzantine Empire.
